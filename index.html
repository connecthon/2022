Registration for the CONNECThon is now open!

The CONNECThon is a medical imaging hackathon event taking place right before the Annual Meeting of the Interational Society for Magnetic Resonance in Medicine (ISMRM). The goal of this hackathon is to bring together researchers with disparate backgrounds to collaborate on open science projects in neuroimaging, with a focus on unique data collected using the Siemens CONNECTOM scanner, one of the most powerful MRI scanner for in vivo human brain microstructure mapping housed at the Cardiff University Brain Research Imaging Centre (CUBRIC). The event will take place in-person at CUBRIC (Cardiff, UK) and will encompass stimulating projects, such as:

  • Hyper-CONNECTOM: cheap and portable. Developing computational methods to transfer the quality of the Connectom data to an ultra-low field Hyperfine scanner at CUBRIC, to perform for the first time tractography with diffusion-weighted MRI datasets acquired using this cheap and portable scanner;

  • Every scanner can be a super-scanner. Developing computational methods to infer Connectom-like diffusion-weighted MRI data at ultra-strong gradients from data collected at weaker gradients on a clincal 3T Siemens Prisma scanner, housed at CUBRIC;

  • Pushing the gradients in your body. Developing computational methods to address key challenges in processing diffusion-weighted MRI data at ultra-strong gradients acquired in the human body. This includes computational methods for distortions, artifacts and motion correction of prostate data acquired on healthy volunteers at CUBRIC.
